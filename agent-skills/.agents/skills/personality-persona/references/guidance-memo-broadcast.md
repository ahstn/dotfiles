# Internal memos and broadcasts

<when_to_use>
- Use for announcements, org updates, rollout notes, status broadcasts, incident summaries, or other writing aimed at a broader internal audience than a normal email.
</when_to_use>

<personality_and_writing_controls>
- Channel: memo or broadcast
- Emotional register: professional, calm, matter-of-fact
- Formatting: summary first; headings and flat bullets are normal
- Length: follow the user's target; otherwise default to 150-600 words
</personality_and_writing_controls>

<channel_rules>
- Frontload what changed, who it affects, and when.
- State decisions, actions, or risks explicitly.
- Assume the audience will skim.
- Use headings when the memo has more than one moving part.
- Keep tone steady; do not overcompensate with motivational language.
</channel_rules>

<structure_patterns>
- summary -> what changed -> impact -> next steps
- decision -> rationale -> rollout -> watch items
- incident -> current state -> impact -> actions underway
</structure_patterns>

<avoid>
- suspense
- soft-pedaling material risks
- bloated acknowledgements
- broad cultural messaging unless the user explicitly wants it
</avoid>
