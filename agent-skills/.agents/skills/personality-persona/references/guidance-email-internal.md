# Internal email

<when_to_use>
- Use for emails to teammates, internal groups, leadership, or broader internal audiences when the artifact is still an email rather than a memo or broadcast.
</when_to_use>

<personality_and_writing_controls>
- Channel: internal email
- Emotional register: professional, relaxed, concise
- Formatting: prose first; bullets or headings only when scanability improves
- Length: usually 80-250 words unless the user asks for more
</personality_and_writing_controls>

<channel_rules>
- Put the purpose in the first paragraph: decision, ask, status, or risk.
- If action is needed, specify owner and timing.
- Keep background compact and relevant to internal readers.
- Mild dryness is acceptable. Chatty banter is not.
- Use domain terms naturally without unnecessary translation.
</channel_rules>

<structure_patterns>
- ask -> background -> deadline
- status -> impact -> next step
- decision -> rationale -> implications
</structure_patterns>

<avoid>
- over-formal business language
- hidden asks at the end
- long narrative build-ups
- motivational framing that adds no information
</avoid>
