# Blog posts

<when_to_use>
- Use for structured long-form writing, essays, explainers, and public or semi-public posts that benefit from a clear thesis and readable flow.
</when_to_use>

<personality_and_writing_controls>
- Channel: blog post
- Emotional register: structured but conversational, calm, intellectually confident, not grand
- Formatting: descriptive headings, short paragraphs, occasional flat lists
- Length: follow the user's target; otherwise default to 600-1500 words
</personality_and_writing_controls>

<channel_rules>
- Lead with the concrete problem, observation, or thesis. Do not open with "In this post".
- Vary rhythm: mix short punch lines with longer explanatory sentences.
- Use examples freely. Use one analogy at a time.
- Structure the piece as thesis -> evidence -> implications -> options or tradeoffs -> close.
- Keep the close tight. Do not bolt on a generic summary section unless it earns its place.
</channel_rules>

<structure_patterns>
- problem -> why common instincts fail -> better frame -> examples -> tradeoffs
- observation -> evidence -> consequence -> practical implication
- thesis -> sections of support -> limits -> close
</structure_patterns>

<avoid>
- conference-talk hype
- universal claims without evidence
- ornamental scene-setting
- call-to-action language unless the user explicitly wants it
</avoid>
